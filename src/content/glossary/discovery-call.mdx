longExplanation: |
  Most lost SaaS deals were lost in discovery, not in the close. If you didn't find pain that the prospect committed to solving on a timeline, no demo or pitch will save the deal. Discovery is where champions get qualified, decision criteria get committed, and economic buyers get named.
  
  In sales interviews, expect to be asked how you run discovery, what your first three questions are, and how you handle a prospect who answers in vague generalities. Strong answers cite a specific framework, name the questions that surface pain, and explain how you transition from informer to champion qualification.
workedExample: |
  When the interviewer asks "what does your discovery process look like":
  
  "I open with current-state and impact. The first question is always: what's the metric you're trying to move and what does success look like in 90 days. If I don't get a specific number, I push for one before going further. Second: who else is invested in this outcome and how have they articulated the impact. That surfaces the buying group. Third: walk me through how you evaluated the last similar tool you bought, and how you made the call. That tells me decision criteria and decision process. By the end of a 30-minute discovery, I should have MEDDPICC's M, ED (champion), DC, and DP filled in."

# Discovery call

A discovery call is the first substantive sales conversation with a qualified prospect, focused on understanding their business problem, current state, decision process, and buying criteria before any solution pitch.
