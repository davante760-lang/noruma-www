longExplanation: |
  MEDDIC originated at PTC in the 1990s and became the dominant qualification framework for enterprise SaaS sales. Most B2B sales orgs that screen on qualification rigor use MEDDIC or its descendants.
  
  If a hiring manager asks you to qualify an opportunity using MEDDIC, you're expected to articulate a metric the buyer cares about, name the economic buyer, list the decision criteria the buying group has committed to, describe the decision process from champion to signature, identify the pain point, and name the champion who carries you internally.

  When asked "qualify a deal using MEDDIC," a strong answer is one paragraph of named specifics. You're not reciting the acronym; you're showing you've applied it to real accounts. Lead with the metric (revenue added, cycle compressed, churn prevented) and end with the champion's title and how they advocated for you internally.

# MEDDIC

MEDDIC is a sales qualification framework: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identified Pain, Champion. It's MEDDPICC's predecessor; MEDDPICC adds Paper Process and Competition.
