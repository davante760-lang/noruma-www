longExplanation: |
  Most enterprise SaaS hiring managers ask candidates to walk a deal end-to-end using MEDDPICC. The framework forces specificity: which metric did you move, who signed the check, what criteria did the buying group commit to, and which competitor did you go up against by name.
  
  MEDDPICC is more than memorization. The hiring signal is whether you've applied it to a real account. Reciting the acronym is table stakes; showing you've used it on a named deal with a quantified outcome is what differentiates.
workedExample: |
  Pretend the interviewer asks you to walk a deal. A MEDDPICC-applied answer looks like this:
  
  Metrics: "We added $1.2M ARR and compressed their average sales cycle from 98 to 61 days. The economic buyer was the CRO, who I met in round three after champion qualification. Decision criteria were vendor consolidation and field-rep time-to-productivity. Decision process was a 4-week eval with our field team plus a security review. Paper process: legal redlines added 9 days. Identified pain: their existing tool's reporting was 12 hours behind real-time. Champion: the VP Sales Ops, who pulled in the CRO. Competition: Salesforce and an internal-build option. We won on time-to-value plus integration depth."
  
  That's MEDDPICC applied. It takes 90 seconds to deliver. Hiring managers will follow up on any of the eight components.

# MEDDPICC

MEDDPICC is a sales qualification framework: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identified Pain, Champion, Competition. It extends MEDDIC with two additional fields (Paper Process, Competition).
