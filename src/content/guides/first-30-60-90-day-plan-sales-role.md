You're in the final round for an AE role. The hiring manager asks: "What
would your first 30, 60, and 90 days look like?"

This question decides whether you get the offer. Nail it, and you prove you
can ramp fast and deliver results. Fumble it, and you look like you haven't
thought about what success means in this role.

Here's how to build a 30-60-90 day plan that wins interviews.

## Why Interviewers Ask for a 30-60-90 Day Plan

They're testing four things:

1. **Do you understand the role?** — Can you articulate what success looks
   like in the first quarter?
2. **Can you ramp quickly?** — Do you have a plan to get productive, or
   will you drift for 3 months?
3. **Are you strategic?** — Do you think in terms of outcomes and
   milestones, or just tasks?
4. **Do you know how to learn?** — Can you ask the right questions, build
   relationships, and absorb context fast?

The worst answers are vague ("I'd learn the product and start
prospecting...") or generic ("I'd meet the team and review the CRM"). The
best answers are specific, milestone-driven, and tied to metrics.

## The 30-60-90 Framework

Break your plan into three phases, each with a clear focus and measurable
outcome.

### Days 1-30: Learn

**Goal:** Absorb context, build relationships, and understand what "good"
looks like.

**What to focus on:**

- **Product knowledge:** Learn the product inside-out. Take the demo, run
  through onboarding, understand use cases and pricing.
- **Market knowledge:** Who are your ideal customers? What pain points does
  your product solve? Who are your competitors?
- **Process knowledge:** What's the sales methodology? What does the tech
  stack look like (CRM, sequencing tools, call recording)? What's the deal
  review cadence?
- **Internal relationships:** Meet your manager, peers, sales engineers,
  CSMs, and product/marketing stakeholders. Learn who to pull in when.
- **Customer conversations:** Shadow 5-10 calls (discovery, demos,
  negotiations). Listen to Gong/Chorus recordings of top performers.

**Outcome metric:**

"By day 30, I'll have completed product certification, shadowed 10 customer
calls, and built a target account list of 50 prospects with research notes
in the CRM."

### Days 31-60: Apply

**Goal:** Start executing with support. Run your own deals under guidance.

**What to focus on:**

- **First outbound campaigns:** Launch prospecting sequences to your target
  account list. Book your first 5-10 meetings.
- **Run discovery calls:** Start qualifying inbound and self-sourced leads.
  Apply the discovery framework you learned in month 1.
- **First demos:** Run product demos (with SE support if needed). Practice
  objection handling in real conversations.
- **Pipeline building:** Get 3-5 qualified opportunities into your
  pipeline. Aim for pipeline coverage of 3-4x your quarterly quota.
- **Feedback loops:** Weekly deal reviews with your manager. Ask for call
  coaching. What's working? What needs to improve?

**Outcome metric:**

"By day 60, I'll have $300k in qualified pipeline (3x my $100k quarterly
quota), completed 15 discovery calls, and run 8 product demos with a 50%
next-step conversion rate."

### Days 61-90: Close

**Goal:** Close your first deals and prove you can hit quota.

**What to focus on:**

- **Accelerate existing pipeline:** Move deals through stages. Handle
  objections, negotiate pricing, navigate procurement.
- **Close your first deal:** Prove you can take a deal from discovery to
  signature.
- **Quota attainment:** Aim to hit 60-80% of your Q1 quota (accounting for
  ramp). If quota is $250k and you're ramped at 60%, aim for $150k
  closed-won.
- **Process refinement:** What's working in your outbound motion? What's
  not? Start building repeatable plays.
- **Contribute to team:** Share a win in team Slack. Help a peer with a
  deal. Bring one actionable insight from a customer conversation to your
  next team meeting.

**Outcome metric:**

"By day 90, I'll have closed $150k in new ARR (60% of my ramped quota),
converted 3 opportunities to closed-won, and identified 2 repeatable plays
for my next quarter."

## Example 30-60-90 Plan (AE Role)

**Scenario:** You're interviewing for an AE role at a Series B SaaS company
selling to mid-market ($50M-$500M revenue companies). Your quota is $1M
annual, $250k per quarter. You're ramped at 60% in Q1.

### Days 1-30: Learn the Business

#### Week 1: Product & Market Immersion

- Complete product certification (internal training, demo walkthroughs)
- Watch 10 recorded demos from top performers (Gong/Chorus)
- Review 5 closed-won deals in the CRM: What was the buyer journey? How
  long was the sales cycle? What objections came up?
- Meet with Product and Marketing: What's the roadmap? What campaigns are
  driving inbound leads?

#### Week 2-3: Customer & Competitive Research

- Shadow 10 customer calls (3 discovery, 4 demos, 2 negotiations, 1 close)
- Build a battlecard for the top 2 competitors
- Interview 2 CSMs: What do customers love? Where do they churn?
- Read 20 customer reviews (G2, Capterra): What themes show up in 5-star
  vs. 3-star reviews?

#### Week 4: Process & Relationships

- Map internal stakeholders: Who owns what? (Sales Ops, RevOps, Solutions
  Engineering, Customer Success)
- Build target account list (50 companies matching ICP)
- CRM hygiene: Set up dashboards, activity logging workflow, and pipeline
  views
- Shadow a deal review: How does my manager think about pipeline health?

**Outcome by Day 30:**

- Product certified
- 50-account target list with enriched notes
- Shadowed 10 calls, reviewed 5 closed deals
- Built relationships with SE team, CSMs, and RevOps

### Days 31-60: Build Pipeline

#### Week 5-6: Launch Outbound

- Build 3 prospecting sequences (pain-point specific: one for sales ops
  leaders, one for RevOps, one for VP Sales)
- Send 150 emails, make 50 calls
- Book 8-10 first meetings
- Run 5 discovery calls (aim for 60% → demo conversion)

#### Week 7-8: Run Demos & Qualify

- Run 6 product demos (3 with SE support, 3 solo)
- Qualify 4 opportunities into pipeline (minimum: $50k ACV,
  multi-stakeholder, clear next steps)
- Handle first pricing objection: Practice value-based pricing, ROI
  positioning
- Weekly 1:1s with manager: Call reviews, objection handling coaching

**Outcome by Day 60:**

- $400k in qualified pipeline (4x my $100k Q1 quota)
- 12 discovery calls completed
- 6 demos delivered, 4 opportunities created
- First pricing negotiation navigated successfully

### Days 61-90: Close Deals

#### Week 9-10: Accelerate Pipeline

- Move 3 deals to "Proposal" stage (send proposals, handle objections,
  schedule executive calls)
- Multithread into buying committee: Engage IT, Finance, and end-user
  stakeholders
- Run ROI workshop with top 2 deals (quantify business impact: time saved,
  revenue protected, cost reduced)

#### Week 11-12: Close First Deals

- Navigate procurement for deal #1 (legal review, security questionnaire,
  MSA negotiation)
- Close first deal: $60k ACV, 2-year contract
- Close second deal: $45k ACV, 1-year contract
- Debrief wins with manager: What worked? What would I do differently?

**Outcome by Day 90:**

- $105k closed-won ARR (42% of my quarterly quota — accounting for ramp
  and timing)
- 2 deals closed, 3 in late-stage pipeline for Q2
- Identified 2 repeatable plays: pain-based sequences for sales ops
  leaders, ROI workshops for CFO objections

## Tailoring Your Plan by Role

### If you're interviewing for SDR

**Days 1-30:** Learn ICP, messaging, and qualification criteria. Shadow 15
discovery calls.

**Days 31-60:** Book 20 meetings (via cold outreach + inbound follow-up).
Qualify 10 SQLs.

**Days 61-90:** Hit 100% of your monthly meeting quota. Hand off 3 deals
that close in your AE's pipeline.

### If you're interviewing for Enterprise AE

**Days 1-30:** Deep-dive into top 10 target accounts (org charts, tech
stack research, news/triggers). Shadow complex, multi-stakeholder deals.

**Days 31-60:** Build champions in 3 target accounts. Run executive-level
discovery calls.

**Days 61-90:** Advance 2 deals into formal evaluation. Goal: $500k+
pipeline by end of Q1.

### If you're interviewing for Sales Manager

**Days 1-30:** Meet your team 1:1 (understand their strengths, gaps, career
goals). Review pipeline health and quota attainment. Shadow team calls.

**Days 31-60:** Run weekly deal reviews. Identify 2 process gaps and
propose fixes (e.g., better discovery framework, competitor positioning
playbook).

**Days 61-90:** Coach team to hit 90% of quota. Close 1 deal yourself
(player-coach). Implement 1 new process improvement.

### If you're interviewing for VP Sales / Director

**Days 1-30:** Audit the full sales org (pipeline health, rep performance
distribution, process bottlenecks, tech stack ROI). Meet cross-functional
leaders (Product, Marketing, CS, Finance).

**Days 31-60:** Identify top 3 leverage points (e.g., "we're losing deals
at the security review stage," "our discovery is weak"). Propose fixes with
exec team buy-in.

**Days 61-90:** Implement 1 high-impact change (new comp plan, updated ICP,
discovery framework rollout). Measure early results. Build trust with your
team.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. No metrics

Saying "I'll build pipeline" without quantifying how much makes you sound
junior. Always include numbers: meetings booked, pipeline created, deals
closed.

### 2. All learning, no execution

Spending 90 days "learning" without closing anything signals you're slow to
ramp. By day 90, you should have closed at least one deal (even if small).

### 3. Generic plan that works for any company

"I'll learn the product and meet the team" could apply to any sales role.
Tailor your plan to this company's ICP, sales cycle, and team structure.

### 4. No relationship-building

Sales doesn't happen in a vacuum. If your plan doesn't mention
cross-functional relationships (SEs, CSMs, Product, Marketing), you're
missing half the picture.

### 5. Overpromising

Saying "I'll close $500k in my first 90 days" when the average sales cycle
is 6 months makes you sound naive. Be ambitious but realistic.

## How to Present Your Plan in the Interview

### Step 1: Ask clarifying questions first

Before diving into your plan, ask:

- "What does success look like for someone in this role at the 90-day
  mark?"
- "What's the typical ramp timeline? When do most AEs close their first
  deal?"
- "What's the biggest challenge new reps face in their first quarter?"

This shows you're thoughtful and gives you context to tailor your answer.

### Step 2: Structure your answer clearly

Use this format:

1. "Here's how I'd break down my first 90 days into three phases: Learn,
   Apply, Close."
2. Walk through each phase (30-60-90) with specific activities and outcome
   metrics.
3. End with: "By day 90, I'd aim to have [specific metric] and be ramped
   to full quota by [timeframe]."

### Step 3: Be specific and quantified

Replace vague language with concrete numbers:

- ❌ "I'd start building pipeline"
- ✅ "I'd aim for $300k in qualified pipeline by day 60, which is 3x my
  quarterly quota"

### Step 4: Show you've done your homework

Reference things you learned in earlier interviews:

- "In my conversation with [Sales Manager], they mentioned the average
  sales cycle is 4-5 months, so I'd focus on pipeline building in the
  first 60 days rather than expecting to close deals immediately."
- "I noticed on your website that you recently launched [new feature]. I'd
  make sure to understand that use case deeply in week 1 since it's
  probably coming up in customer conversations."

## Example Answer (Full Delivery)

**Interviewer:** "What would your first 30, 60, and 90 days look like in
this role?"

**You:**

"Great question. Before I answer, can I ask—what does success look like for
a new AE at the 90-day mark? And what's the typical ramp timeline?"

**Interviewer:**

"Most reps close their first deal around day 75-90. We ramp quota at 60% in
Q1, so if your full quota is $250k per quarter, you'd be aiming for $150k
in Q1. Pipeline coverage should be 3-4x quota."

**You:**

"Got it. Here's how I'd structure my first 90 days—three phases: Learn,
Apply, Close.

**Days 1-30: Learn the business.** I'd focus on product knowledge, customer
conversations, and internal relationships. Specifically: complete product
certification, shadow 10 customer calls (discovery, demos, negotiations),
review 5 closed-won deals in the CRM, and build a target account list of 50
companies matching your ICP. By day 30, I'd have that account list loaded
in the CRM with research notes and be ready to start prospecting.

**Days 31-60: Build pipeline.** I'd launch my first outbound campaigns—3
pain-based sequences targeting sales ops leaders, RevOps, and VP Sales
personas. Goal is to book 10-12 first meetings, run 8-10 discovery calls,
and qualify 4-5 opportunities into pipeline. By day 60, I'd aim for $400k
in qualified pipeline, which is 4x my $100k Q1 quota, and I'd have run at
least 6 product demos with a 50% next-step conversion rate.

**Days 61-90: Close deals.** I'd focus on moving deals through the
pipeline—handling objections, running ROI workshops, navigating procurement
and security reviews. My goal would be to close 2 deals by day 90, totaling
around $100k-$120k in ARR, which gets me to about 60-70% of my ramped
quota. I'd also identify 2 repeatable plays—like a pain-based sequence
that's working or an objection-handling approach—that I can scale in Q2.

By day 90, I'd be fully ramped, pipeline-healthy, and contributing to the
team."
