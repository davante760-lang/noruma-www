    <p>Anchor your prep on three numbers from public data: base of $100k–$160k, OTE of $111k–$210k, and a sample of 3 verified Atlassian reps on Levels.fyi. Map your previous comp to those bands. If you're stretching above the band, have a story for why your impact justifies it; if you're under, frame the gap as room to grow into. Recruiters at Atlassian will ask your number in the first call. Have it ready, anchored to public data, with one sentence on why you're worth the upper end.</p><p><strong>Behavioral questions: STAR with numbers.</strong> Bring three deal stories. One closed-won with a named outcome metric (revenue added, cycle compressed, churn prevented). One closed-lost with the specific moment it tipped (when did your champion go dark, who else got involved, what would you do differently). One active opportunity you're multi-threading right now to prove current pipeline, not just history. Lead with the customer name and the dollar size. Skip the setup, get to the action and the result inside two sentences.</p><p><strong>Deal walkthroughs: MEDDPICC, applied not recited.</strong> Most Atlassian hiring managers will ask you to walk a deal end-to-end. Be ready to articulate the metric you moved, the economic buyer's name and title, the decision criteria the buying group committed to, the decision process they ran, the paper-process from procurement to signature, the identified pain, the champion who carried you internally, and the competition (named, not vague). Don't quote the acronym. Show you've used it on specific accounts. The framework is the structure; your stories are the evidence.</p><p><strong>Pipeline questions: bring metrics, not adjectives.</strong> When they ask how you build pipeline, have a multi-channel motion documented in your head: percent from inbound, outbound, partner-sourced. Have a weekly cadence number. Have a connect-rate. Have a meeting-to-opportunity conversion. If you don't track these numbers in your current role, start tracking them this week. They will be asked, and "high activity" without numbers is not a good answer.</p><p><strong>Forecast accuracy: bring a specific percentage.</strong> The hiring manager round will probe how often you call your number on day one of the quarter. Have a quarterly accuracy rate ready, e.g. "within 10 percent of called number, four quarters in a row." If your accuracy is weak, name it and explain what you changed. Honest answers about misses with a real lesson beat polished claims that don't withstand probing.</p><p><strong>Multi-threading: name the people.</strong> Atlassian hiring managers will probe whether you actually multi-thread or just rely on a single champion. Have a specific deal where you mapped 5+ stakeholders, the order in which you reached each, and what triggered each new contact. Vague answers about "building relationships across the org" fail. Specific named-person stories pass.</p><p>For early-cycle qualification, BANT (budget, authority, need, timing) still gets asked at Atlassian, especially in the first hiring-manager round. Give a quick, honest answer with a real example attached, not a textbook recitation.</p>
