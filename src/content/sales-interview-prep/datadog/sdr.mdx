    <p><strong>Activity is the table stakes question.</strong> SDR interviews at Datadog probe weekly call volume, connect rates, meetings booked per month, and conversion of those meetings to qualified pipeline. Have specific numbers ready: a daily call target you hit, a weekly meeting count, and your AE-meeting-to-opportunity ratio. Vague answers (\"high activity\") fail; specific answers (\"75 dials per day, 12 meetings booked per month, 60 percent meeting-to-opportunity\") pass.</p>
    <p><strong>Qualification is the differentiating question.</strong> Datadog operates in a technical buyer landscape. Engineering leaders, DevOps managers, and SRE teams are the personas. SDRs who walk in with a generic BANT response (budget, authority, need, timing) will not stand out. Show you can probe technical pain: \"What does your monitoring stack look like today? How do you handle distributed traces across microservices? Where are your blind spots when an incident hits 2am?\"</p>

You don't need to be a deep technical expert, but you should be able to explain what each Datadog product does in 30 seconds and which buyer persona cares about it. Tie this to the discovery questions you'd ask in a first call.</p>
    <p>For the panel and mock-call rounds, expect a roleplay where you're handed a fictional account and asked to qualify. Lead with technical curiosity over pitch-mode. The hiring signal is whether you sound like someone who'd partner well with the AE you'll be paired with on accounts.</p>
